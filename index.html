Conway's Game of Life 4 Axioms to Conway's Game of Life

  1. Any live cell with fewer than two live neighbors dies, as if caused by under-population.
  2. Any live cell with two or three live neighbors lives on to the next generation.
  3. Any live cell with more than three live neighbors dies, as if by overcrowding.
  4. Any dead cell with exactly three live neighbors becomes a live cell, as if by reproduction.

Please refer to the wikipedia link to see what this is all about. There is a lot to it, and it has interesting implications to evolution, biological life, and mathematics. But... simply click on 'Randomise' and 'Start' to begin the simulation. Some patterns do more interesting things than others. The wikipedia page will tell you about them if you don't want to hunt.
